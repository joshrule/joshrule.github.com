Josh Rule

CV · GitHub · rule@berkeley.edu

I am a postdoctoral scholar with Steven Piantadosi and Alison Gopnik at UC, Berkeley.

My research uses behavioral experiments and computational methods to study how people develop conceptual systems and leverage them to accomplish their goals. My work revolves around an idea called the child as hacker—that symbolic programs provide the most compelling account of sophisticated mental representations and that learning is analogous to a particular style of programming called hacking. I am particularly interested in understanding how human programmers write code–all the goals, activities, and tools people use to make code better–and applying these insights to better describe learning in children and adults.
